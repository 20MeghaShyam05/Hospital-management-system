# Patient Services Guide

## Purpose
This guide explains how patients should use the Doctor-Patient Appointment Management System for registration, appointment booking, rescheduling, cancellation, queue visibility, and AI-assisted help.

## Patient Registration Rules
- Every patient must register with a valid full name, email address, and 10-digit mobile number.
- The patient UHID is the preferred hospital-facing identifier for future visits and support.
- A patient may log in using patient ID, UHID, or registered email.
- The initial patient password is the registered mobile number until it is changed.

## Patient Login and Security Policy
- Patients must keep their passwords private and should change the initial password after first login.
- Patients are allowed to access only their own appointments and personal profile data.
- A patient must not view or edit another patient's records.
- Password changes require the current password and a new replacement password.

## Appointment Booking Policy
- A patient may book an appointment only against an available slot for the selected doctor and date.
- The slot must belong to the selected doctor and the selected appointment date.
- Weekend appointments are not allowed through normal booking flow.
- Past-date appointments are not allowed.
- Emergency priority may be selected when the case requires faster queue handling.

## Appointment Reschedule and Cancellation Policy
- A patient may cancel an appointment only with a valid cancellation reason.
- A short or blank cancellation reason is not acceptable.
- The released slot returns to availability after successful cancellation.
- Rescheduling is limited by system policy and must remain with the same doctor.
- A patient must select a valid new slot that belongs to the same doctor and the new date.

## Queue and Visit Expectations
- Emergency appointments receive higher queue priority than normal appointments.
- Queue status may be shown as waiting, in-progress, completed, no-show, or cancelled.
- Patients should arrive before the appointment time to reduce queue delays.
- If the patient does not arrive, the visit may be marked as no-show by staff.

## AI Assistant Boundaries for Patients
- The AI assistant provides navigation help, doctor-routing suggestions, and general self-care guidance.
- The AI assistant does not diagnose disease and does not prescribe medicines.
- If symptoms sound urgent, the assistant must advise immediate emergency care.
- AI responses should be treated as support guidance, not as final medical advice.

## Patient Self-Care Policy
- Self-care suggestions must remain low-risk and general.
- The system should not advise medicine dosages or treatment plans.
- Severe chest pain, breathing difficulty, stroke-like symptoms, heavy bleeding, confusion, or collapse require urgent care immediately.

## Support Process
- For login issues, patients should verify their identifier and password before contacting admin support.
- For booking issues, patients should re-check the selected doctor, date, and slot.
- For queue issues, patients should contact the reception desk or hospital admin.
