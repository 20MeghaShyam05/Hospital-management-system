# Admin Operations Manual

## Purpose
This manual defines the operational rules for hospital admins using the Doctor-Patient Appointment Management System.

## Admin Scope Policy
- Admins may register patients and doctors.
- Admins may manage scheduling, appointments, queue operations, and reports across the full hospital scope.
- Admins may access role-aware AI assistance for workflow guidance and report interpretation.
- Admin credentials must be protected and used only by authorized staff.

## Data Governance Policy
- Admins must use official patient and doctor identifiers whenever possible.
- Admins must not create duplicate records when an existing patient or doctor already exists.
- Admins must validate email, mobile number, and role-linked identifiers before registration.
- Admins are responsible for ensuring operational data reflects real hospital events.

## Doctor Registration and Scheduling Policy
- Doctor registration must include specialization, contact details, work hours, and consultation duration.
- The system auto-generates future weekday availability for newly registered doctors.
- Manual availability must not duplicate existing availability for the same doctor and date.
- Slot generation must respect work hours, consultation duration, and lunch-break blocking rules.

## Appointment Operations Policy
- Admins may book appointments for valid patients against available doctor slots.
- Admins must confirm that the selected slot belongs to the selected doctor and date.
- Emergency priority may be assigned only when operationally justified.
- Past-date and weekend booking attempts must be rejected.
- Cancellation requires a meaningful reason.
- Rescheduling must comply with doctor/date/slot consistency and reschedule-limit policies.

## Queue Operations Policy
- Emergency appointments must be moved ahead of normal appointments in queue priority.
- Queue actions must follow valid status progression and should reflect real patient flow.
- Completed and no-show outcomes should be recorded promptly to keep reports accurate.

## Reporting Policy
- Reports should be generated only for valid operational dates, not future dates.
- Admins should use reports to monitor appointments, completions, cancellations, no-shows, and utilization.
- Natural-language report summaries generated by AI must stay grounded in actual report data.

## RAG Knowledge Base Policy
- Policy and process answers should be grounded in approved hospital knowledge documents.
- Knowledge documents must be versioned and curated by admin-approved authors.
- Embeddings should be stored in a separate pgvector-backed Postgres instance when possible.
- Retrieved context must be limited to the current user's operational role when role-specific guidance is requested.

## Incident and Escalation Policy
- If the API, database, queue, or reporting flow is inconsistent, admins should verify the backend health first.
- If LLM or retrieval services fail, the UI should fall back to standard operational workflows without blocking core booking functions.
- Admins should treat AI output as advisory and verify any operationally important guidance before acting on it.
